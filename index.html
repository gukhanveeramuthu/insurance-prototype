Insurance Payment &
Refund Validation System

An end-to-end NEFT lifecycle engine that automates premium receipting, validates bank accounts via penny drop, and dispatches refunds with integrity guardrails — eliminating manual errors and fraud risk.

Problem
  • Manual NEFT receipting causes errors and processing delays
  • Refunds risk failure and fraud due to unvalidated bank details
Solution
  • Automated NEFT ingestion directly into core system
  • Penny drop + fuzzy name match validation before any refund
Impact
  • Refund TAT cut from 30 → 7 days
  • Receipting accuracy improved significantly
  • Fraud risk reduced via automated guardrails

Key Product Decisions
Tradeoffs made during design — this is where PM thinking is visible
1
Implemented penny drop verification despite added cost to ensure bank account authenticity and reduce fraud risk
The ₹1 cost per validation is negligible compared to the risk of a failed or fraudulent refund. Fraud prevention value far outweighed the operational cost.
2
Introduced configurable name match threshold (e.g. 90%) to balance strict validation with approval rates
A hard fixed threshold would block too many legitimate refunds or pass too many mismatches. Configurability gives ops teams control to tune fraud risk vs approval rate dynamically.
3
Designed system-driven workflows to minimize dependency on manual intervention by underwriters
Previously UW tagging required manual steps at every stage. Automating proposal-to-receipt mapping removed the bottleneck and cut processing TAT significantly.
4
Ensured refund processing is restricted to verified premium payer accounts only
Refunds can only be dispatched to the account that originally sent the premium payment — preventing misdirected payouts even in edge cases like changed account details.
